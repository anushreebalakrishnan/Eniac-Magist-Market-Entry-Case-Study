# ENIAC & Magist: Brazil Market Entry Case Study
The main objective is to evaluate whether ENIAC should enter the Brazilian market through a 3-year partnership with Magist. 

## Overview

This case study evaluates whether **ENIAC**, a high-end technology company, should enter the **Brazilian market** through a partnership with **Magist**, a Brazilian service company that offers a centralized order management system.

The analysis focuses on determining whether Magist is the right strategic partner for ENIAC’s expansion into Brazil.
---

## Business Objective

ENIAC plans to expand into the **Brazilian market within the next year**.

### Challenge

ENIAC lacks sufficient knowledge of:

- Brazilian customer behavior
- Market dynamics
- Logistics and delivery systems
- Local operational structure

To overcome these challenges, ENIAC considered signing a **3-year contract with Magist**.

---

## About Magist

Magist is a Brazilian service company that provides:

- Centralized order management
- Stock and warehouse management
- Product shipment
- Customer support related to deliveries

### Key Facts about Magist

- Covers the entire Brazilian territory
- Well-established company
- Over **99,000 orders** in the last two years
- More than **70 product categories**
- Benefits from economies of scale

---

## Main Areas of Investigation

The study focused on two major concerns:

### 1. Magist Expertise
**Is Magist the right partner for ENIAC?**

The analysis evaluated whether Magist has sufficient expertise in handling **high-tech and premium electronic products**, which are central to ENIAC’s business model.

### 2. Magist Operations
**Can Magist meet ENIAC’s service standards?**

The evaluation focused on:

- Delivery performance
- Customer satisfaction
- Shipping efficiency
- Operational reliability

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Weak High-Tech Product Alignment

The study identified that Magist lacks strong specialization in **high-tech products**, which raises concerns about compatibility with ENIAC’s premium technology portfolio.

---

### 2. Customer Price Segment Mismatch

Magist customers mainly purchase **lower-priced products**, while ENIAC targets the premium electronics segment.

| Concept | ENIAC | Magist |
|----------|--------|---------|
| Average Item Price | €540 | €110 |
| Average Order Value | €710 | €140 |

This indicates a significant difference in customer purchasing behavior.

ENIAC products are approximately **5 times more expensive** than products commonly sold through Magist.

---

### 3. Limited Premium Customer Base

Only **3% of Magist customers belong to the “Very Expensive” segment**, suggesting limited alignment with ENIAC’s premium customer profile.

---

### 4. Operational Performance Concerns

The operational assessment showed that Magist’s performance remains **below ENIAC’s standards** in terms of:

- Delivery speed
- Customer satisfaction
- Order handling performance

This raises concerns regarding long-term partnership effectiveness.

---

## Final Recommendation

### Recommendation: Do Not Sign a 3-Year Contract with Magist (Current Form)

Based on the findings, the recommendation is:

❌ **Avoid signing the proposed 3-year agreement with Magist in its current structure.**

### Suggested Alternatives

- Identify an alternative partner that better aligns with ENIAC’s business standards and premium market positioning.
- If no suitable alternative exists, renegotiate the partnership as a **yearly contract** instead of a long-term commitment.

This approach would reduce risk and provide greater operational flexibility.

---

## Conclusion

Although Magist offers strong market reach, operational infrastructure, and logistics support in Brazil, there is a clear mismatch between:

- ENIAC’s premium technology positioning
- Magist’s customer purchasing behavior
- High-tech expertise requirements
- Expected operational standards

Therefore, a long-term strategic partnership presents considerable business risk.
